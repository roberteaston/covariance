Let $S : V \to V$ and $T : V \to V$ be linear transformations that commute, i.e. $S \circ T = T \circ S$. Let $v \in V$ be an eigenvector of $S$ such that $T(v) \ne 0$. Prove that $T(v)$ is also an eigenvector of $S$.
